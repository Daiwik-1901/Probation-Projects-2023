Frequently Asked Questions

A cupcake is a small, individual-sized cake that is typically baked in a paper or aluminum cup. It's a popular dessert or snack often enjoyed at parties and special occasions.

Store cupcakes in an airtight container at room temperature for a day or two. For longer storage, you can refrigerate them, but be sure to bring them to room temperature before serving to maintain their texture.

Use quality ingredients, don't overmix the batter, and bake at the recommended temperature and time. You can also add buttermilk, yogurt, or sour cream to keep them moist.

You can use natural sweeteners like honey or applesauce and reduce the sugar content in your cupcake recipes for a healthier option.

Using a piping bag with different tips allows you to create various frosting designs. You can practice on a flat surface before decorating the cupcakes.
